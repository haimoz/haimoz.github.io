Novel Visual Experiences

applications of binocular rivalry in HCI

With 3D TVs, VR, and AR, stereoscopic 3D contents are commonplace nowadays. These technologies can show different images to each individual eye, allowing for the presentation of arbitrarily different image pairs that do not represent 3D objects in the physical world. This creates a phenomenon called binocular rivalry, based on which we created several interesting visual effects, and a technique to help color blind users tell apart confusing colors.

In the BeyondStereo project, we explored several interesting visual effects by presenting different images to each eye. Below is a demo video in side-by-side 3D format (no sound). For the best experience, you can:
