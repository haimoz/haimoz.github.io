MHair

novel haptic feedback by magnetically activating augmented hair

Of the many technologies for delivering haptic feedback, we have not seen one that specifically stimulates the hair only, without stimulating the skin. We found a way to do this, which is potentially associated with affective touch, the type of touch that links to emotional responses ranging from soothing to thrilling.

The way to do it is quite intuitive:

  1. paint the hair with metallic particles (which, guess what, is already in use in many off-the-shelf cosmetics!)
  2. pull the augmented hair around with a magnetic field, such as one from a permanent magnet or from an electro-magnet

We used an off-the-shelf cosmetic that contains metallic particles, mixed it with hair gel, and apply the mixture to the hair with a mascara brush. We then mounted a permanent magnet on a CNC router, which moves the magnet around and in turn pull the augmented hair towards different directions.
